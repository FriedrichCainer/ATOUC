Gig: Unidentified maintenance obstruction

Prize: $8 Reward Points: 1000

A grainy night-vision video played at double speed as a small repair bot navigated the roof cavity between apartment floors. It wove between bundles of cabling until it reached the drainage pipe the video had highlighted with a green box.

Home Automation -> Apartment 3709 -> Bathroom 2 -> Sink Hot Water Tap -> 500ml

A rainbow splash from the infrared overlay showed the water pouring down the pipe and pooling midway, a slow blue trickle dribbling down the other half. The next twenty minutes was a fast forward montage of the robot draining the pipe.

An articulated drill/hose combination snaked out and bit into the pipe to drain it of the cooling water. Next was a small saw appendage which methodically cut a tiny window into the side of the pipe that was opened up like a hatch. The night-vision scene was then blinded as it switched on its lights, flooding the gruesome scene with a splash of real color.

Blockage identification, confidence insufficient, <98%
Organic - Vermin - Rat (Deceased) - 96.87%
Inorganic - Household - Toy - 55.46%
Organic - Human Waste - Hair - 47.12%
Inorganic - Fashion Accessory - Wig - 12.03%

Jonah was 100% certain that was a dead rat. He selected the dropdown option for the rat, drew a square around the area where it was visible on the screen and submitted the task for completion.

Please select all images that show “Organic - Vermin - Rat (Deceased)”

A 10x10 grid of images was displayed. Most were of other unfortunate rats in various states of decay. Jonah dutifully selected those and clicked Submit.

Somewhere in the world unseen by anyone but Jonah the tiny maintenance bot could now continue with the standard process for [Organic Waste removal and incineration] followed by [Sterilization and Pipe section replacement]. Globally, all of its sibling bots now had a new trick and future unfortunate damp rats would be seen and mourned by nobody.

Congratulations!
Classification confidence for “Organic - Vermin - Rat (Deceased)” is now at 100%!
We have rewarded your account a bonus 500 points, keep up the *fantastic* work!

Jonah did a drum solo on the head of the bed behind him. That got him over the line with 240,000 points for the month! He transferred ⅔ of the points into his hardware subscriptions; his tier was far from cutting-edge but high enough that it kept him working and in the game.

He converted the rest into various online currencies, subscriptions and unlock codes that he needed for the month. These he handed out as prizes to his fans. Jonah’s real passion was streaming; he had a small but growing fanbase who watched him play video games while delivering his witty banter and commentary. Jonah was adamant that becoming a streaming star was his only shot at moving out of his father’s house.

The cash balance was just for the occasional fast food delivery and he only bothered to order that so he could expand his “butterfly collection”. He was happy to eat “Healthy Essentials” for most of his meals and that came included as part of his citizen package but once he had enough saved he’d order a fried mushroom box or cereal with icy-milk.

Once the drone was at the window he’d catch it with a net he’d made from old telephone cables someone had stored on huge reels in a lot nearby. At first he’d been quick to wrap the drone’s body in tinfoil like he’d read online so the companies couldn’t track what had happened to it. Nowadays of course the drones were clearly getting so disposable that nobody seemed to care if they went missing.

Each new addition he pinned to the wall with old kitchen knives or cooking skewers he’d run through the rotor ring. They quickly stopped moving but occasionally a light would flicker or a rotor would briefly attempt to spin as those without the tinfoil received a software update.

As he was quietly admiring his collection his door burst open. “What did I fucking tell you about drumming on the wall like that!”

Jonah threw his hands up in a combination of surprise and protest. “Dad I…”

“I have work in 4 hours, work at a *job*, something which-”

“I have a job too!” Jonah interjected.

“-you’d never under-, what? What fucking job?”

“I told you, I’m a plumber!”

His father’s eyes looked like they wanted to burst from their sockets in fury.

“A plumber! You’re not a fucking plumber, you’ve never so much as touched a wrench in your life let alone gotten your hands dirty. What you’re doing on that computer is the opposite of plumbing; your generation is the death of plumbing.”

“It’s a job and it pays so -”

“It’s not even minimum wage.”

“Dad, minimum wage hasn’t existed in decades, that’s something you lot-”

His father simply turned and slammed the door behind him. Jonah sat on his bed, heart hammering in his chest. These fights had been escalating for months. His father worked in a job that represented a final vestigial organ of the modern economy that Jonah grew up in. Full-time, protected by a generous redundancy and pension, his father had in his comfort unwittingly surrounded himself with a soothing media bubble that pitted him against his son.

Starting innocently enough, his father had liked and shared stories and memes that conformed to his pre-determined demographic. Eventually he was fed a version of the news that described a world of the same opportunities that he had grown up with. The irony was the same had happened to his parents, turning them slowly into fearful ideologues in their final years.

Each morning he’d read the news and together with millions his own age railed against the basic income his son subsisted on. In the pseudo-anonymous comments sections they identified the dispossessed and poor, including their own children as the root cause of the problems they faced in their lives.

There was no mastermind behind the scenes or name for the movement. It was just yet another emergent combination of unhappy middle-aged people, mostly men, amplified by fully automated vested interests.

The door burst open again.

“That’s it, you need to start paying rent. Starting tomorrow you need to pay me $4500 and again on the first of each month.”

Jonah simply laughed. “What planet are you from? I don’t know *anyone* my age who has that kind of money.”

“Bullshit, I heard your friend Kevin is making decent money down at -”

“Kevin! Kevin wasn’t my friend and he was top of the class, he was the very best we had and all that got him was some deadbeat coder job. Actually you know what -” Jonah rummaged around on his bed for a tablet and with a few taps turned to show the screen to his Father. “Ha, as I expected, Kevin moved to Reboot 6 months ago!”

“Those communists? Ok, why don’t you join them then? You’d like that, you already live on benefits and don’t work, I’m sure they’d love to have you.”

“Communists? Dad, seriously, stop reading your recommended news feed, you have no idea what’s going on. Anyway, they wouldn’t have me, the visas are only for people like Kevin.”

“Well there you go, finally now after leaving you have a reason to pay attention at school.”

“I’ve seen your report card, Dad. Do you realise that I would have been a valedictorian in your age? This is all life has for my generation. You’re either the 1 in 100 who get to go to Reboot or you’re stuck with basic citizen package and gigs.”

His father paused for long enough to give Jonah hope, but then he saw it, the shutdown. Whenever he’d fight with his father they’d reach a point where the facts were stacked against him, where he had to choose between his simple media bubble narrative of a fair meritocracy that made sense and his son. Jonah could always see the exact moment where he’d reset, shake off the doubts and start again.

“Well maybe if you’d stopped playing your fucking video games for 5 minutes you’d-”

Jonah ripped one of the drones from the wall and flung it at his father. He was stuck back in his bubble. The drone flew through the air like a frisbee before the rotors spun up and brought it to a standstill inches from his father’s face. Pausing to assess the situation before flying out the open window it cheerfully sang “Icy Nicey Cereal, enjoy your meal!”

Jonah slumped against the wall and nervously laughed at the drone’s ridiculous departure. He looked up at his father, expecting that it had defused the situation, but instead he was looking back at Jonah in absolute fury. Jonah looked down at the large kitchen knife in his hand which had pinned the drone to the wall.

“You threaten me with a knife in my own home?”

“Dad, I -”

“I want you gone. Tonight.”

With that he left and within an hour of packing up his life so had Jonah.
